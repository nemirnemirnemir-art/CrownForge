## Strongest Individual Cards (Community)

### Demon's Altar (King of Blood)
- [UNCONFIRMED] Considered one of the most powerful cards in the game
- Reason: 0.5% growth per dead ally is permanent and compounds across ALL battles
- In a 33-year run with consistently dying imps, this scales infinitely
- Strategy: Pagoda/Cemetery spam Imps to die and feed the Altar

### Mycelium (King of Nature)
- [UNCONFIRMED] Also considered top-tier
- Exact mechanic [MISSING] but community agrees it's extreme scaling
- [INFERRED] Likely multiplies or amplifies adjacent card output

### Lab Rat (King of Progress)
- [CONFIRMED] No level cap
- As adjacent plots are upgraded, Lab Rat levels up without limit
- Combined with Concabulator (insta-level everything) this creates extreme burst scaling

### Dragon's Den (King of Nomads)
- [CONFIRMED] +20% per destroyed plot
- Nomads actively destroy plots via Razing or Migration to feed this
- With Pillage decree: reportedly "broken" in late game

## Strongest Decrees (Community)

### Catharsis
- [CONFIRMED] Effect: all troops deal damage equal to your highest-damage troop
- [UNCONFIRMED] Considered game-changing; equalizes weak troops to your strongest
- Best if you have one extremely powerful troop (Lab Rat, Mercenary with lots of gold, etc.)

### Overwork + Blacksmith
- [CONFIRMED] Overwork: support buildings apply effects one extra time
- Combined with Blacksmith (which buffs adjacent troops): each Blacksmith now buffs twice
- [UNCONFIRMED] Considered extremely strong combo for King of Nothing

### Feast (×stacked)
- [CONFIRMED] Doubles HP per stack; stackable 20 times
- 20× stacks = 2^20 HP multiplier = theoretical 1,000,000× base HP
- [INFERRED] In practice limited by encounter scaling, but high stacks are dominant

### Bunker (×stacked)
- [CONFIRMED] +50% tower attack speed per stack; stackable 20 times
- Tower-focused builds (Stone especially) benefit enormously

## Strongest Combos (Community)

### Blacksmith + Farm (King of Nothing)
- Early-game engine: Farm provides gold; Blacksmith buffs adjacent troops
- [UNCONFIRMED] Considered best early build for beginners

### Demon's Altar + Pagoda + Cemetery (King of Blood)
- Pagoda summons Imps; Cemetery revives dead units as Imps; each dead Imp feeds Altar
- Creates a self-sustaining growth loop
- Weak early (Imps are fragile), very strong mid/late

### Lab Rat + Concabulator (King of Progress)
- Concabulator insta-levels all plots to lvl 3 → Lab Rat jumps multiple levels at once
- Repeat Concabulator activations = Lab Rat reaches absurd power levels
- [MISSING] Whether Concabulator can be reused or is one-time

### Dragon's Den + Pillage + Migration (King of Nomads)
- Intentionally destroy plots via Razing/Migration
- Each destroyed plot = +20% to Dragon's Den permanently
- Pillage decree amplifies the destruction strategy
- [UNCONFIRMED] "Broken" in late game at high stacks

### Library + Enchantment spam (King of Spells)
- Library duplicates enchantments on adjacent cards
- Enchantments have no stack limit
- Stack unlimited enchantments for extreme stat inflation

## General Strategic Principles (Community Distilled)

1. **Identify your win condition by Year 4** — the Royal Decree at Year 4 often defines the run
2. **Level 3 before breadth** — upgrading fewer cards to level 3 is usually better than spreading
3. **Adjacency is free value** — always consider what's next to what
4. **Tower builds are safe** — towers can't die; Stone king with Bunker decree is consistently reliable
5. **Don't ignore the Merchant (Year 12)** — save some gold for the shop
6. **Diplomat matters** — choosing war/peace incorrectly can shut out card pools you need
7. **Blood king timing** — don't evaluate Blood king's power by Year 10; it snowballs after Year 15+

## Difficulty Curve

- Years 1–10: Forgiving; most kings can survive with suboptimal play
- Years 11–20: Synergies need to click; weak builds start losing battles
- Years 21–32: Build must be functional and scaling; random deaths become rare
- Year 33: Fully optimized build required; punishes anything unfinished
- Endless: [INFERRED] Each cycle harder; designed for extreme builds only
