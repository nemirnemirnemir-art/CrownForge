# 00 — Overview

## Genre

**9 Kings** is a roguelike kingdom builder combining:
- **Deck-building** — collect cards after each battle, build your kingdom loadout
- **City-building** — place cards on a grid to construct a kingdom
- **Auto-battler** — kingdoms fight automatically each year

Developer: Farbflut Entertainment | Platform: Steam (Early Access as of research date)

---

## Confirmed Facts

### Structure
- [CONFIRMED] Run = sequence of "years"; each year = one battle + one card pick
- [CONFIRMED] 9 playable kings, each with a unique 9-card deck (including 1 Base card)
- [CONFIRMED] 2 enemy-only kings: Rainbow King and Chaos King
- [CONFIRMED] Standard run ends at Year 33 (boss fight); endless mode continues cycling every 33 years
- [CONFIRMED] Losing a battle costs 1 life; losing the Final Battle (Year 33) removes ALL lives
- [CONFIRMED] Win condition: survive Year 33

### Grid
- [CONFIRMED] Starting grid: 3×3 = 9 slots
- [CONFIRMED] Tower event grants 2 additional plots; further plots cost 30g each after Year 33
- [CONFIRMED] Normal max: 11 slots; with perks: up to 13 slots

### Cards
- [CONFIRMED] 6 card types: Troop, Building, Tower, Enchantment, Tome, Base
- [CONFIRMED] Cards upgrade to level 3 by placing a duplicate on the same plot
- [CONFIRMED] Towers cannot be attacked, destroyed, or enchanted
- [CONFIRMED] Enchantments have no stack limit

### Meta-progression
- [CONFIRMED] 78 total perks (earned between runs)
- [CONFIRMED] 9 perk slots by default; expandable to 11–15

## What Makes This Game Distinct

1. **Grid placement is strategic** — adjacency bonuses, synergy between card types
2. **Asymmetric kings** — each king plays completely differently (not just stat variations)
3. **Post-loss rewards** — losing a battle gives you a card from your OWN deck (consolation draft)
4. **Indestructible towers** — towers never die, creating a permanent infrastructure layer
5. **Stacking Enchantments** — no cap creates extreme late-game scaling
6. **Decree selection** — Royal Decrees at Year 4 are often run-defining choices
